# Galley UI Direction

## Experience posture

Galley should feel like an editorial command desk: composed, operational, premium, and built for accountable client work. It is a supervision surface, not a prompt box or an AI spectacle.

## Visual system

The interface uses an ink, bone, and brass palette. Ink Black anchors the shell, Charcoal holds working surfaces, Graphite creates precise rules, and warm Paper and Bone carry text and document content. Galley Brass is the signature accent. Proof Blue is reserved for digital interaction clarity; red, green, and yellow communicate system state only.

Surfaces should be opaque and restrained. Fine borders, strong typography, and generous spacing replace glassmorphism, glowing gradients, and decorative effects. Editorial serif display type can establish hierarchy, while the product interface remains grounded in a clean grotesk and compact monospaced labels.

## Core product surface

The proof queue is the central screen. A reviewer should see the client, deliverable, channel, verifier result, status, and accountable actions without reconstructing context. Approve, edit, reject, and view record are explicit. The human gate is always visible, and approval never appears synonymous with publishing.

## Trust artifact

The record is Galley’s chain of custody. It shows the ordered history from playbook selection through generation, verification, human edits, approval, scheduling, and publishing. Events read as immutable evidence, with clear actors and timestamps. Prototype records must be labelled as static until persistence exists.

## Hierarchy and space

Each screen communicates one principal idea. Headlines are direct, sections breathe, and status is treated as a meaningful design element. Product evidence takes priority over illustration. Dense information is disclosed progressively rather than packed into a dashboard grid.

## Motion

Motion is limited to soft fades, short slide transitions, precise hover feedback, and calm state changes. Avoid bouncing icons, sparkles, fake typing, cursor effects, and animated gradient decoration.

## Copy guardrails

Use short sentences, exact nouns, and observable states. Galley may describe what was generated, checked, approved, or recorded. It must not use fake performance metrics, generic AI promises, autonomous-publishing claims, or dashboard theatre.
