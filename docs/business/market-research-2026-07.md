## 1. Market Size

### Content marketing / content marketing software
- Global content marketing industry revenue: ~$63B (2022), projected to reach **$107B by 2026** (Statista, statistic 527554).
- Content marketing software (narrow SaaS category): **$33.1B (2024) → $44.7B (2030), 5.1% CAGR** (Research and Markets, 2025). Alternate: $12.14B (2025) → $22.55B (2030), 13.2% CAGR (Mordor Intelligence, 2025). Maximize Market Research (2024): $44.1B by 2030 at 18.4% CAGR. **Flag:** sources diverge widely due to segmentation differences — cite the range, not one number.
- Technavio (2021): content marketing to grow by $417.85B during 2021–2025 at ~16% CAGR (incremental growth figure, not end-state market size).

### AI content creation
- **Generative AI in content creation: $14.8B (2024) → $80.12B (2030), 32.5% CAGR 2025–2030** (Grand View Research).
- Digital content creation market: $32.28B (2024) → $69.80B (2030), 13.9% CAGR (Grand View Research, 2024).
- Narrower "AI-powered content creation tools": $2.14B (2024) → $7.81B (2032), 17.6% CAGR (DataM Intelligence, 2024); similar from Maximize Market Research ($2.10B 2023 → $6.54B 2030).

### Agency market (Galley's buyer universe)
- Global advertising agencies market: **$444.7B in 2025**, +2.7% YoY (IBISWorld, Global Advertising Agencies, 2025).
- US advertising agencies: **$78.2B market (2025); 114,014 businesses (2026)**, +4.4% YoY (IBISWorld, report 1433).
- US digital advertising agencies (subset): **$53.4B market (2025); 100,202 businesses (2026)**, +14.9% YoY; 16.6% average annual business-count growth 2021–2026 (IBISWorld, report 5889). Fastest-growing, most fragmented, most tool-buying segment — core TAM denominator for Galley.
- US agency revenue per employee: ~$220,000 (2025) (RevenueMemo, 2026 — secondary aggregation, directional).

### Brand governance / compliance (closest conceptual competitors)
- **Writer.com**: Starter $29/mo; Enterprise custom. Benchmark contracts: 10–50 users $10K–$50K/yr; 100–500 users $75K–$250K/yr; large deals >$500K/yr (eesel AI, 2026; Vendr 2026). Style guide + terminology + claims detection for regulated industries. Enterprise-only motion — SMB agencies unserved. Strongest evidence that claims/brand enforcement commands 5–6 figure ACVs.
- **Acrolinx**: enterprise content governance. **Flag: no public pricing — custom quotes**; consistently described as high-five/six-figure enterprise (ContentMation 2026).
- **Grammarly Business**: ~$15/user/mo annual; negotiated $10–15 at 10–200 seats (Vendr 2026; CostBench 2026). Style guides + brand tones. Real-time suggestions — no workflow, no publish gate, no audit record.

### Social approval / scheduling
- **Sprout Social**: Professional ~$299/seat/mo (approval workflows); Advanced ~$399/seat/mo (Ampifire, 2025). Per-seat cost makes multi-approver agency use expensive — a wedge for Galley.
- **Hootsuite**: Professional $99/mo (1 user); Team $249/mo (3 users, approvals); Business $739/mo; Enterprise custom with compliance/audit at top tiers (Searchlab 2026). Approvals gated behind $249+/mo.
- **Buffer**: from $6/channel/mo; Team $12/channel/mo adds approvals (Searchlab 2026).
- **Later**: Scale $82.50/mo annual (4 users, 48 profiles); no true multi-step external-client approval (Superdirector, 2026).

**Whitespace summary:** AI writing tools have no governed approval loop; approval tools (Gain, Planable) have no AI verification or claims compliance; claims-compliance tools (Writer, Acrolinx) are enterprise-priced and not agency-shaped. No found competitor combines AI draft + playbook verification + human publish gate + append-only audit at agency-affordable ($100–500/mo per client-workspace) pricing.

## 3. Demand Signals

### AI adoption by marketers
- 51% of marketers using or experimenting with generative AI, +22% planning soon (Salesforce, 2023–25 series); **87% of marketers use generative AI in at least one workflow in 2026, up from 51% in 2024** (Salesforce State of Marketing 2026 — **flag: secondary citation, verify against the report**).
- 91% of marketing leaders say their teams use AI (HubSpot AI Trends for Marketers, 2025).

### Accuracy / hallucination / brand-safety pain
- **47% of marketers encounter AI hallucinations weekly; 36.5% admit hallucinated content has been published; >70% spend hours weekly fact-checking AI output** (NP Digital study, via PPC Land / MartechEdge, 2025).
- >70% of advertisers have had an AI-related incident; 40% paused/pulled ads; ~33% suffered brand damage; <35% plan to increase AI-governance investment (IAB, 2025).
- Suspected AI content reduces reader trust by ~50% (Raptive, July 2025); consumers saying heavy AI use reduces brand trust rose 20% (2025) → 39% (2026) (eMarketer Brand Safety 2026).
- ~60% of US digital advertising professionals avoid placement next to content containing inaccuracies/hallucinations (eMarketer, 2026).

### Regulatory pressure
- **FTC "Operation AI Comply"** (Sept 2024, continuing through 2025): 12+ actions under FTC Act Section 5 against unsubstantiated AI/marketing claims — DoNotPay settled $193K (Jan 2025); Workado (Apr 2025) for 98% accuracy claims vs ~53% actual (FTC 2024–25; Benesch Law, 2025).
- **FTC Fake Reviews Rule** (final Aug 14, 2024): civil penalties up to **$51,744 per violation** for fake/AI-generated reviews and testimonials (FTC, 2024; Alston & Bird, 2024).
- Updated FTC Endorsement Guides (June 2023) extend substantiation to digital/social and AI-assisted claims (Morgan Lewis, 2023).

### Google / SEO
- Google penalizes "scaled content abuse" (March 2024 core update), not AI content per se; quality judged by E-E-A-T; human review/editorial accountability is the practical mitigation (Google Search Central, 2023–24).

### Approval bottlenecks
- **89% of marketers report 3+ approval stages; 58% say >40% of time goes to reviews/approvals** (Storyteq, 2025/26 — **flag: vendor research**).
- 52% of companies often miss deadlines due to approval delays (Marq/zipBoard, 2025 — vendor-sourced, directional).
- 71% of marketers expect content demand to grow 5x by 2027 (Adobe, 2023).
- Agencies reclaim est. 10–15 hrs/client/month from streamlined approvals (SocialPilot, 2025 — vendor claim, directional).

## 4. Business Model Benchmarks

- **Pricing models:** per-seat dominates AI writing (Jasper $59–125/seat) and social (Sprout $299–399/seat); **per-client-workspace pricing proven in the agency niche** (Planable $33–49/workspace/mo; Gain $99/mo per 6 clients). Convention: unlimited reviewers free, charge by client/brand count.
- **Gross margin:** SaaS benchmark 75–80%+; blended median 77% (Benchmarkit 2025; KeyBanc/Sapphire 2024). AI-inference COGS pressures this; budget below median initially.
- **Churn:** SMB SaaS monthly churn 3–7% (31–58% annualized); mid-market 1.5–3%; SMB vs mid-market logo churn 6.4% vs 2.8% (Optifai benchmark of 939 B2B SaaS companies, 2025; Vena 2025). Martech churn skews high (Focus Digital, 2025). Plan 25–40% annual gross churn in SMB-agency; audit-record data gravity is the retention lever.
- **CAC/LTV:** average B2B SaaS CAC $1,200–$2,000 (referral ~$150; outbound ~$1,980); SMB LTV $15K–$40K; median LTV:CAC 3.2:1, target ≥3:1 (Optifai 2025; SaaSHero 2026). Median B2B SaaS ACV $62K (KeyBanc 2024 — skews enterprise; agency SMB ACVs ~$1.2K–$12K).
- Martech sales cycle: ~2 days median for low-ACV self-serve (GetMonetizely 2025 — directional).

## 5. Willingness to Pay

- Agencies spend ~3.7% of revenue on tools/software (RevenueMemo, 2026). At ~$220K revenue/employee ⇒ roughly **$8,100/employee/year (~$675/mo) software spend** — **flag: derived from two stats, not directly surveyed**.
- Demonstrated price points for approval/workflow alone (no AI, no compliance): Gain $99–199+/mo (6–12 clients); Planable $33–49/workspace/mo; Hootsuite $99 → $249/mo jump specifically to unlock approvals; Sprout $299/seat/mo. A 10-client agency on Planable Pro ≈ $490/mo; 3 Sprout seats ≈ $900/mo.
- Claims/brand governance: Writer $10K–$50K/yr even at 10–50 seats; Acrolinx enterprise-custom. **Nothing exists between Grammarly Business ($15/seat, no workflow) and Writer ($10K+ floor)** — the $200–$1,000/mo gap is Galley's pricing corridor.
- Cost-of-failure anchors: $51,744 per FTC fake-review violation (FTC 2024); 40% of advertisers pulling ads after AI incidents (IAB 2025); >70% of marketers spending hours/week fact-checking (NP Digital 2025) — at $100–200/hr agency rates, 5 hrs/week of QA = $2,000–$4,000/mo of displaceable labor.
- **Flag:** no public survey directly measures agency willingness to pay for content QA as a category; corridor is triangulated. Primary customer-discovery interviews required.
